Mathematical Amnesia

Theorem libraries remember every success. Cutset remembers what failure taught us.

Research campaigns lose their negative knowledge. A formulation fails, someone explains why in a meeting or chat, and six months later another person or agent tries it again. The failure produced real information, but the system discarded it.

Cutset makes that information load-bearing. Every failed route becomes a wall with three fields: what died, the constraint extracted from its death, and the surviving route that inherits the constraint.

The Compiler

Turn an open problem into a computable frontier.

Cutset is a small, honest compiler over a certificate graph. It walks backward from a goal through theorem-grade routes, subtracts killed formulations, follows surviving routes, and emits the minimal open leaves still separating the campaign from closure.

01 · KEEP POSITIVES

Proved routes

Every theorem-grade edge carries exact hypotheses and provenance. The graph knows what can legally transport into the goal.

02 · BANK NEGATIVES

Walls

Killed formulations never re-enter silently. Their constraints persist and their named survivors inherit the narrowed search space.

03 · EMIT RESIDUAL

The cut-set

What remains is loadable, recomputable, and spawnable as bounded research contracts. It is a frontier, never a proof.

The hard law: Cutset cannot promote OPEN to THEOREM. Graph composition is reachability plus honesty tags. Only Lean, lake, or another named deterministic gate changes mathematical status. A model may propose. The checker decides.
